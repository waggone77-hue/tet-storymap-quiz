Historians estimate the combined death toll of the Tet Offensive (including communist forces, U.S. and South Vietnamese troops, and civilians) was:
Estimates vary, but historians combining communist, U.S., South Vietnamese, and civilian deaths generally put the Tet Offensive's death toll at over 80,000 people. Rough figures include more than 58,000 communist troops killed by the end of the offensive, nearly 3,900 U.S. dead, about 5,000 South Vietnamese soldiers killed, and roughly 14,000 civilians who died in the fighting.
