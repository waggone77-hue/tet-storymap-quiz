During the Vietnam War, which U.S. intelligence agency was primarily responsible for the collection of human intelligence (HUMINT)?
The CIA was the lead U.S. agency for HUMINT in Vietnam, running networks of agents, informants, and covert programs. Fun fact: CIA officers in Saigon and the field worked so closely with South Vietnamese services that some operations, like the controversial Phoenix Program, blurred the lines between intelligence, policing, and counterinsurgency.
